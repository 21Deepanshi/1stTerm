Introduction

Virat Kohli is an Indian international cricketer (born on 5 November 1988) and also a captain of the Indian national cricket team. Kohli is widely regarded as one of the greatest batsmen in the history of cricket and the best of the 21st century. He holds the record as the highest run-scorer in T20I and IPL, ranks third in ODI, and stands as the fourth-highest in international cricket. Virat Kohli is a king of cricket. (Wikipedia, 2024)

Achievements

  • Kohli has made 80 centuries which includes 7 double centuries in international cricket.
  • 29 centuries including 7 double centuries in Test cricket.
  • 50 centuries in One Day Internationals (ODIs) surpassing the record of Sachin Tendulkar
  • Virat Kohli becomes the first Indian cricketer to cross 10,000 T20 runs.
  • He is the only Indian batsman who got 890 rating points in the ICC ODI ranking.
  • He became the fastest ever to complete 26,000 runs in International cricket.
  • Virat won the T20I series in South Africa, England, New Zealand, and Australia.
  • Fastest Indian and second fastest in the world to reach 10, 15, 20, and 25 centuries in ODIs.

Recent games

He was named in India's squad for the 2023 Cricket World Cup, his fourth appearance in the tournament.But unfortunately, he was not able to succeed, which was very painful time for the nation. In the opening match against Australia, India were at 2 for 3, with the top order collapsing again similar to India's previous WC match against New Zealand in the 2019 WC semi-final. In response, he made a brilliant 85 in a partnership of 165 runs with KL Rahul as India chased the target. He made a century against Bangladesh and a 95 against New Zealand. (Wikipedia, 2024)
